Why I Became Both an Electrician and a Developer: Bridging Two Worlds

Most people choose one career path and stick with it. I chose two. As an A-Grade licensed electrician and a full-stack developer, I've built a unique career that bridges traditional trades and modern technology. Here's why I made this choice and how it benefits my clients in Melbourne.

The Beginning: A Traditional Trade

My journey started in the electrical trade. I became an A-Grade licensed electrician because I wanted a practical, hands-on career where I could see the immediate impact of my work. There's something satisfying about wiring a house, installing smart home systems, or fixing an electrical issue that's been plaguing a business.

Working as an electrician taught me valuable skills:

  • Problem-solving under pressure - When a power outage hits at 2 AM, you learn to think quickly and find solutions
  • Attention to detail - Electrical work requires precision; one mistake can be dangerous
  • Understanding how things work - From residential wiring to industrial systems, I learned the fundamentals of how technology powers our world
  • Customer service - Working directly with homeowners and business owners taught me how to communicate complex technical concepts in simple terms

The Pivot: Discovering Web Development

While working as an electrician, I noticed something interesting: many of my clients needed more than just electrical work. They needed websites, online presence, and digital solutions. I started learning web development to help these clients, and I discovered I had a natural aptitude for it.

The transition wasn't as strange as it might seem. Both fields require:

  • Logical thinking and problem-solving
  • Following best practices and safety standards
  • Understanding systems and how components work together
  • Continuous learning as technology evolves
